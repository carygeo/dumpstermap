How to Rent a Dumpster

Your complete step-by-step guide to renting a roll-off dumpster for home renovations, cleanouts, and construction projects

Home → How to Rent a Dumpster

Renting a dumpster for the first time can feel overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be. Whether you're tackling a home renovation, cleaning out a garage, or managing construction debris, this guide walks you through the entire process in 6 simple steps.

What you'll learn:

  • How to choose the right dumpster size for your project
  • What to look for when comparing rental companies
  • How to prepare your property for delivery
  • Common mistakes to avoid (and how to save money)

Estimated time: 30 minutes to research and book. Total cost: $250-$600 depending on size and location.

1

Determine What You're Throwing Away

Before you start shopping for a dumpster, make a list of what you'll be disposing of. This affects both the size you need and the type of dumpster you should rent.

Common accepted materials:

  • Household junk and furniture
  • Construction debris (drywall, lumber, flooring)
  • Roofing shingles
  • Yard waste (branches, leaves)
  • Appliances (without refrigerants)
⚠️ Prohibited items: Most dumpsters do NOT accept hazardous waste, paint, batteries, tires, electronics, or appliances containing refrigerants. Ask your provider about their specific policies.

If you're disposing of heavy materials like concrete, brick, or dirt, you'll need a "heavy debris" dumpster with different weight limits and pricing.

2

Choose the Right Dumpster Size

Dumpster sizes are measured in cubic yards. Here's a quick guide to help you choose:

Size Capacity Best For Avg Price
10 Yard 3-4 truck loads Single room cleanout, small garage $250-$400
15 Yard 4-5 truck loads Medium cleanout, deck removal $275-$425
20 Yard 6-8 truck loads Basement cleanout, medium renovation $300-$450
30 Yard 9-12 truck loads Major renovation, new construction $400-$550
40 Yard 12-16 truck loads Commercial, whole-house cleanout $450-$600
💡 Pro tip: When in doubt, size up. Overage fees for exceeding weight limits or needing a second dumpster usually cost more than renting the next size up.

Need help deciding? Use our dumpster size calculator or read the complete size guide.

3

Compare Local Providers

Don't just book the first company you find. Prices and service quality vary significantly between providers. Here's what to compare:

  • Base price – What's included in the quoted price?
  • Rental period – Most offer 7-14 days; check for extension fees
  • Weight allowance – Typically 2-6 tons depending on size
  • Overage fees – Usually $50-$100 per ton over limit
  • Delivery/pickup fees – Often included, but verify
  • Customer reviews – Check Google, Yelp, or BBB ratings
💡 Save time: Use DumpsterMap to see all providers in your area at once, complete with ratings, services, and contact info.
4

Book Your Dumpster

Once you've chosen a provider, call or book online to schedule delivery. Be prepared to provide:

  • Your address and preferred placement location
  • Dumpster size needed
  • Delivery date and rental duration
  • Type of debris you'll be disposing of

Before you hang up, confirm:

  • Total price (including delivery, pickup, and disposal)
  • Weight limit and overage charges
  • Rental period and extension fees
  • Prohibited items policy
  • Cancellation policy
💡 Best time to book: Schedule 2-5 days in advance for the best availability. During peak seasons (spring and summer), book 1-2 weeks ahead.
5

Prepare Your Property for Delivery

Proper preparation ensures smooth delivery and protects your property:

  • Clear the space: You need at least 10 feet wide × 25 feet long
  • Move vehicles: Clear the driveway completely
  • Check overhead: Trim branches and watch for power lines
  • Ensure truck access: Delivery trucks need 60+ feet to maneuver
  • Protect your driveway: Place plywood sheets where the dumpster will sit
⚠️ Permits: If your dumpster will be placed on a public street (not your driveway), you may need a permit from your city. Many providers can help with this.

On delivery day, be available by phone in case the driver needs guidance. Most drivers will call when they're en route.

6

Fill the Dumpster and Schedule Pickup

Now the easy part—fill it up! Follow these guidelines to avoid problems:

  • Load evenly: Distribute weight throughout the container
  • Stay below the fill line: Items cannot extend above the top edge
  • No prohibited items: These can result in refused pickup or extra fees
  • Break down large items: Collapse boxes, disassemble furniture if possible

When you're finished, either call your provider to schedule pickup or they'll collect on your agreed end date. Some providers offer early pickup if you finish ahead of schedule.

💡 Running out of space? If you realize mid-project you need more room, call your provider immediately. It's often possible to schedule an early pickup and get a second dumpster.
